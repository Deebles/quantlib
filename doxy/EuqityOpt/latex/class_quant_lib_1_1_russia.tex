\subsection{Detailed Description}
Russian calendars. 

Public holidays (see {\tt http\+://www.\+cbr.\+ru/eng/}\+:)\+: 
\begin{DoxyItemize}
\item Saturdays 
\item Sundays 
\item New Year holidays and Christmas, January 1st to 8th 
\item Defender of the Fatherland Day, February 23rd (possibly moved to Monday) 
\item International Women\textquotesingle{}s Day, March 8th (possibly moved to Monday) 
\item Labour Day, May 1st (possibly moved to Monday) 
\item Victory Day, May 9th (possibly moved to Monday) 
\item \doxyref{Russia}{p.}{class_quant_lib_1_1_russia} Day, June 12th (possibly moved to Monday) 
\item Unity Day, November 4th (possibly moved to Monday) 
\end{DoxyItemize}

Holidays for the Moscow Exchange (M\+O\+EX) taken from {\tt http\+://moex.\+com/s726} and related pages. These holidays are {\itshape not} consistent year-\/to-\/year, may or may not correlate to public holidays, and are only available for dates since the introduction of the M\+O\+EX \textquotesingle{}brand\textquotesingle{} (a merger of the stock and futures markets).
